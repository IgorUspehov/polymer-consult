Stretch Film Agglomeration: Complete Setup Guide

Date: April 24, 2024 | Author: Ihor Kryazhev, Technology Consultant

1. Why agglomeration is necessary

Stretch film has a bulk density of only 30-80 kg/m³. The extruder cannot pick up such light material. Agglomeration densifies the film to 500-550 kg/m³ through friction and heating. Without this step, pelletizing is impossible.

2. Agglomerator design

The agglomerator consists of a chamber with 2-4 rotating knives (400-600 rpm), a stator, and a friction heating system. The knife gap is 0.3-0.5 mm. The service life of European D2 steel knives is 300-600 tons.

3. Parameters for clean stretch film (LLDPE)

  • Heat to 120-130°C in 4-6 minutes
  • Water addition 1-2%
  • Agglomerate density 500-550 kg/m³
  • Extruder temperature 160-220°C
  • Filtration 200-250 microns
  • Output 80-150 kg/h (45 mm extruder)

4. Knives: service life and sharpening

Chinese knives last 80-150 tons, European D2 steel knives 300-600 tons. The knife gap of 0.3-0.5 mm must be checked regularly. If the gap exceeds 1 mm, productivity drops by 30-40%.

5. Typical mistakes and solutions

  • Agglomerate falls apart: underheating (<110°C) or too little water (<1%). Increase cycle time.
  • Black spots: overheating (>150°C) or polymer sticking to knives. Clean knives.
  • Strong odor: contaminated material. Washing required before agglomeration.

6. Conclusion

Agglomerator+screw is the optimal solution for stretch film up to 300 kg/h. Temperature and knife control ensure stable pellet quality.
